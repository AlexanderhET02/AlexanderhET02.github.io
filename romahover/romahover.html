The Teatro di Marcello, or Theatre of Marcellus, is an ancient open-air theater in Rome, Italy, built during the late Roman Republic. Originally conceived by Julius Caesar, construction was completed in 13 BC under Emperor Augustus, who dedicated it to his late nephew Marcus Claudius Marcellus. As the largest and most important theater in Ancient Rome, it could accommodate between 11,000 and 20,000 spectators and was constructed using tuff, concrete, and travertine stone. The theater featured three levels of arches, with Doric columns on the lower tier, Ionic in the middle, and possibly Corinthian on the upper level. Over time, it fell into disuse and was repurposed as a fortress in the medieval period before being transformed into the Palazzo Savelli-Orsini in the 16th century.
